Sustainable cities must effectively acquire and deliver critical provisions such as food, water, security, and energy to their residents. While producing resources locally is an important solution, cities must also reach far beyond their borders to secure resources.

Resource provisioning requires coordinated approaches that understand the interaction among delivery approaches, the interdependencies of decisions that affect supplies of these resources, and stakeholder risks. Aging infrastructure must also be addressed to ensure that critical provisions are delivered.

Illinois CURES will cut across the silos that have traditionally impeded the effectiveness of a nexus approach to resource provisioning.
