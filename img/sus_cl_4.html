Citizens of urban areas desire the economic prosperity necessary to afford a high standard of living. Cities must provide sufficient opportunities for employment and wealth creation. However, prosperity need not be synonymous with continual growth. Instead, cities must find ways to create sustainable economic growth that does not damage the environment through pollution or poor land use planning. Cities with sustainable growth will be the most sought communities for job creation as well as urban development.

Illinois Cures?research on sustainable urban prosperity will identify strategies for accomplishing economic advancement that mitigate the damage done to the urban environment. We will also address other employment concerns, such as understanding the changing fields of employment and job training needs.
