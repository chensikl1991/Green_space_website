Climate change is threatening cities worldwide. From sea level rise to flooding, wildfires, drought, and insect infestation, climate change is challenging cities?ability to meet residents?basic needs. Climate change can impact the quality of drinking water, increase vector-borne diseases, create the need for more temporary housing, and strain cities?ability to manage stormwater. Often the vulnerable populations within cities suffer most from the effects of climate change.

Cities must learn to prepare for and respond to these challenges. Strategies may include understanding medium and long-term weather trends, developing robust emergency plans, increasing the amount of greenspace, and establishing cooling centers.

Cities must also prepare for and respond to population fluctuations. In-migration stresses the capacity of a city to provide employment and basic resources for the expanded population, while out-migration leaves cities scrambling to respond to struggling economies and abandoned properties.

Illinois CURES will develop research-based strategies and multi-pronged approaches to make cities more resilient to these changes.
